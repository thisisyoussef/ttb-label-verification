# Stitch Screen Brief

## Story

- Story ID: `TTB-001`
- Title: single-label reviewer workflow and evidence surfaces

## Screen goal

Produce the core single-label review workstation flow: intake, processing, results, standalone mode, and the dense government warning evidence surface.

## Target user moment

A reviewer needs to upload one label, optionally compare it with application data, and understand the result immediately without learning a complex interface.

## Screen prompt for Stitch

Design a utilitarian government compliance workstation for TTB label verification. Show a two-column intake screen with image upload on the left and optional application data on the right, a clear processing state, and a results experience centered on a recommendation banner, checklist rows, expandable evidence, and a special government warning detail view. Use large readable text, restrained color, dense but calm layout, and zero marketing styling.

## Required sections and components

- intake screen with upload zone and optional application form
- processing screen with progress steps and image preview
- results screen with recommendation banner, counts, checklist rows, and expandable details
- standalone mode treatment
- government warning detail surface
- new review and export actions

## Required states to visualize

- empty intake
- uploaded image ready
- processing
- mixed pass/review/fail results
- standalone mode
- low-confidence warning state

## Copy anchors and terminology

- `TTB Label Verification`
- `Application Data`
- `Verify Label`
- `Results`
- `Government Warning Detail`
- `New Review`
- `Run Full Comparison`

## Constraints and must-avoid notes

- no cards-inside-cards aesthetic
- no decorative gradients, blobs, or consumer-brand feel
- no tiny body text
- keep the warning detail readable and structured, not a wall of text
- preserve a digital-checklist feel rather than a dashboard-for-the-sake-of-a-dashboard
