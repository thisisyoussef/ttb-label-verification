# Stitch Screen Brief

## Story

- Story ID: `TTB-004`
- Title: accessibility, hardening, and submission pack

## Screen goal

Produce the release-polish pass for the integrated product: trust messaging, accessible dense states, calm failure handling, and final evaluator-facing readiness.

## Target user moment

A reviewer or evaluator is using the integrated product and needs it to feel stable, legible, and serious enough for real compliance work.

## Screen prompt for Stitch

Design the final polish pass for an integrated TTB label verification workstation. Focus on readability, accessible status signaling, error banners, low-confidence guidance, empty states, and clear dense warning evidence. Keep the existing industrial workstation character and refine it for trust, legibility, and evaluator confidence rather than reinventing the product.

## Required sections and components

- polished single-label result states
- polished batch dashboard states
- low-confidence and error banners
- empty and filtered states
- trust-note surfaces where needed

## Required states to visualize

- low-confidence result
- no-text-extracted error
- timeout or processing issue
- empty filtered dashboard state
- dense expanded warning detail at high readability

## Copy anchors and terminology

- `Results`
- `Batch Results`
- `Government Warning Detail`
- `Try Again`
- `Replace File`
- `Reset Filters`

## Constraints and must-avoid notes

- do not redesign the app hierarchy
- preserve the approved single-label and batch structures
- improve readability and trust without adding decorative treatment
- keep status understandable without relying on color alone
